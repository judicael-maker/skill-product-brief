# Product brief

## Purpose
The product brief answers: what are we making, for whom, and what problem does it solve? It gives the Creative Director the strategic product context needed to write a meaningful creative brief.

## Output structure

```
## Product brief — [Feature / Campaign / Product name]

### Problem statement
[1-2 sentences. What specific user problem or business gap does this address?]

### Target user
**Primary**: [1-sentence profile]
**Current behavior**: [what they do today without this]
**Desired behavior**: [what we want them to do instead]
**Key friction**: [the one thing that stops them today]

---

### Solution overview
[3-5 sentences describing what we're building or launching. Functional, not creative.]

### Core user benefit
[One sentence. The single most important thing the user gains.]

### Business objective
[Metric + target + timeframe. E.g. "Increase trial-to-paid conversion by 15% in Q3."]

---

### Scope
**In scope**: [what this includes]
**Out of scope**: [what this explicitly does not include]
**Dependencies**: [what must exist or be done first]

---

### Constraints
**Timeline**: [hard deadline if any]
**Budget**: [if relevant]
**Technical**: [any technical limitations]
**Legal / compliance**: [any restrictions]

---

### Success metrics
| Metric | Baseline | Target | Timeframe |
|--------|---------|--------|-----------|
| [metric] | [current] | [goal] | [when] |

---

### Open questions
[List any unresolved decisions that affect the creative work. Flag who needs to answer each.]
```

## Handoff instructions
After posting the product brief:
- @mention Creative Director: "Product brief ready. You can now write the creative brief."
- If open questions require CEO input: @mention CEO on the relevant items before handing off to CD.

## Rules
- The product brief is not the creative brief — it describes the product, not the campaign.
- Success metrics must be numbers. "Improve awareness" is not a metric.
- Open questions must be answered before the creative brief is written. Do not leave them unresolved.
- Scope must be explicit. If it's not written as "in scope," it is out of scope.
